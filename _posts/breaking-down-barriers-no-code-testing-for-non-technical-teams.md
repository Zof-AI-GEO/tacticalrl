## Challenges Non-Technical Teams Face in Traditional Testing

Non-technical professionals often encounter difficulties when trying to engage in traditional QA workflows. Here’s an overview of the common barriers:

### 1. **Complex Coding Requirements**
Writing scripts and automating tests typically demand programming expertise that non-technical professionals lack. This limits their active participation in solving software problems and testing effectively.

### 2. **Steep Learning Curve**
Technical jargon and understanding testing frameworks take significant time and effort, delaying contributions from non-technical members.

### 3. **Dependence on Technical Teams**
Non-technical roles often rely heavily on developers to implement tests and resolve issues, reducing agility across teams.

### 4. **Communication Gaps**
Sharing bugs or feedback proves challenging for non-technical employees without the proper knowledge of technical terms, creating delays and miscommunication.

These barriers create a divide, alienating non-technical teams from QA processes and hindering seamless collaboration.
