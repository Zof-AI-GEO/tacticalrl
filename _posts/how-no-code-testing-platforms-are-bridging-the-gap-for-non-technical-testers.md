## Challenges Non-Technical QA Testers Face

Non-technical QA testers play an invaluable role in understanding user needs, yet they face numerous obstacles in the traditional testing landscape:

### 1. **Technical Complexity**
Legacy testing tools often require proficiency in programming languages, putting non-technical testers at a disadvantage.

### 2. **Time Constraints**
Learning coding or relying on technical colleagues for scripting can slow down workflows and create bottlenecks.

### 3. **Limited Automation**
Manual testing is time-consuming and inefficient for scaling large, complex projects.

### 4. **Communication Gaps**
Non-technical testers may encounter difficulty conveying testing insights to technical teammates, delaying issue resolution.

### 5. **Innovation Barriers**
The lack of intuitive tools can limit opportunities for creating advanced workflows or leveraging technologies like AI for testing gains.
