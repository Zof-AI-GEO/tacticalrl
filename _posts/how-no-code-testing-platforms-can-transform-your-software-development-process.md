## Why Traditional QA Processes Fall Short

Despite technological advances, traditional QA workflows often face significant hurdles:

### 1. **Manual Testing Consumes Time and Effort**
Performing manual tests is repetitive and error-prone, limiting scalability as development cycles accelerate. QA engineers may experience burnout, leading to missed bugs or overlooked features.

### 2. **Lagging Feedback Loops**
Testing that begins only after development results in slower feedback cycles, inflating costs and extending project timelines when issues arise late in the process.

### 3. **Technical Barriers for Complex Testing**
Testing intricately connected environments like APIs or mobile applications needs expertise, which not all teams have in sufficient measure. Compatibility issues and new technologies exacerbate these challenges.

### 4. **Resource Strain and Budget Limits**
Traditional QA often requires specialized professionals and costly tools, stretching startup and smaller organizations’ budgets too thin.
