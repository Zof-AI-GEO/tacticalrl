## Understanding Agile Methodology in Software Testing

The agile approach emphasizes iterative development, continuous feedback, and customer focus. Projects are divided into "sprints," where specific functionalities are developed and tested, integrating testing as a continuous process rather than a one-off end task. This methodology ensures:

- **Faster defect detection**: Bugs are identified during development, reducing costly post-production fixes.
- **Enhanced collaboration**: Facilitates teamwork among developers, testers, and business stakeholders.
- **Swift delivery**: Adapted for rapid deployment without sacrificing quality.

However, manual testing methods often struggle to keep up with agile’s fast-paced cycles, creating a gap that no-code testing platforms effectively fill.
