### Challenges of Traditional QA Processes

Historically, QA relied heavily on code-based frameworks like Selenium and Appium. While functional, these methods come with several complications:

1. **Technical Expertise Required**:
   - Traditional testing demands expert coding skills, limiting who can effectively create and maintain test cases.
   - Non-technical stakeholders cannot contribute efficiently, creating bottlenecks.

2. **Time-Consuming**:
   - Writing test scripts is a painstaking process; any application updates typically require reworking these scripts.

3. **Accumulation of Technical Debt**:
   - Scripts grow outdated or poorly optimized over time, requiring significant effort to maintain.

4. **Costly Processes**:
   - High expertise levels translate into higher salaries and operational costs.

In the fast-paced world of agile development and CI/CD (Continuous Integration/Continuous Delivery), these issues only compound. There’s an urgent demand for an agile, accessible, and cost-efficient QA solution.
