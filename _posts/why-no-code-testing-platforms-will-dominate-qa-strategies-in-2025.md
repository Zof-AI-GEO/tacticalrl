## The Flaws of Traditional Testing Frameworks

Legacy testing systems are riddled with inefficiencies, slowing down time-critical software deployments. Here’s a closer look at the existing challenges:

### 1. **Complexity Slowing Down Innovation**
Traditional testing tools demand advanced coding knowledge, creating dependency on experienced developers and diminishing efficiency. This leaves teams grappling with longer timelines and bottlenecked workflows.

### 2. **Limited Cross-Functional Inputs**
Coding-intensive frameworks often exclude involvement from non-technical stakeholders like product managers or designers, missing out on comprehensive team insights.

### 3. **Scalability Issues as Projects Grow**
As software complexity grows, maintaining scripts for vast projects becomes costly and cumbersome, leading to potential delays and productivity losses.

### 4. **Labor-Intensive Maintenance**
Manually updating scripts as applications change is resource-heavy, with teams spending more time on maintenance and less on improving product performance.
