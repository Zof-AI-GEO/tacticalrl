## The Struggles of Traditional Testing Methods

### Manual Testing: Slow and Prone to Errors
Manual QA has long been standard practice, but its inefficiencies are glaring in today’s fast-paced development world:

- **Time-Consuming:** Testers take weeks to manually review every function and edge case, delaying releases.
- **Repetitive and Error-Prone:** Monotony increases human error, leading to missed bugs.
- **Lacks Scalability:** Complex apps demand more testing, overwhelming manual teams.

### Coded Testing: Technically Complex
Automated testing tools like Selenium and Appium were revolutionary but come with limitations:

- **Requires Coding Expertise:** Teams need skilled developers to write and maintain test scripts.
- **High Maintenance:** Scripts break with updates to UI or functionality, consuming time and resources.
- **Expensive and Steep Learning Curve:** Training new hires to use complex tools is costly.

Both manual and coded testing struggle to keep up with agile DevOps workstreams, highlighting the need for a no-code solution.
